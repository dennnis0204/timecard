What are your data protection rights?

TIMECARD  would like to make sure you are fully aware of all of your data protection rights. Every user is entitled to the following:

1. The  right to be informed – this means we must inform you how we are going to use your personal data. We do this through this privacy policy.

2. The  right of access – you have the right to access your personal data (e.g. data that is about you) that we hold. This is called a subject access request.

3. The  right to rectification – if you think the data we hold on you is incorrect, tell us so we can put it right.

4. The  right to erasure – you have the right to request that we delete your data. We will do so, provided that we do not have a compelling reason for keeping it.

5. The  right to restrict processing – you have the right to request that our Service restrict the processing of your personal data, under certain conditions.

6. The  right to data portability – you have the right to request that our Service transfer the data that we have collected to another organization, or directly to you, under certain conditions.

7. The  right to object to processing – you have the right to object to our Service processing of your personal data, under certain conditions.

If  you make a request, we have one month to respond to you. If you would like to exercise any of these rights, please contact us at our email timecard.contact@gmail.com

Cookie Policy

This  Cookie Policy was last updated on July 20, 2019

Cookies  are small text files stored by your browser as you browse the internet. Cookies allow us to remember things about your visits to TIMECARD, like your preferred language, and to make the site easier to use.

The  law states that we can store cookies on your device if they are strictly necessary for the operation of this site. For all other types of cookies we need your permission.

We  use both session cookies, which expire after a short time or when you close your browser, and persistent cookies, which remain stored in your browser for a set period of time. We use session cookies to identify you during a single browsing session, like when you log into TIMECARD.

Necessary  cookies help make a website usable by enabling basic functions like page navigation and access to secure areas of the website. The website cannot function properly without these cookies.
